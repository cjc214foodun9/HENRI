1. Coherence Loss ($\mathcal{L}_{coherence}$): Wavefront Isomorphism

We don't care if the absolute magnitude drops due to attenuation; we care exclusively about the shape of the thought. Therefore, we flatten the complex spatial tensors and use Cosine Similarity to calculate the geometric distance between the ideal FP32 wave and the physical 4-bit wave.

$$\mathcal{L}_{coherence} = 1.0 - \frac{1}{N} \sum \cos(\theta_{ideal}, \theta_{physical})$$
2. Thermodynamic Clamp Loss ($\mathcal{L}_{thermo}$): The Hardware Limit
If the Fourier Translation Engine aligns too many constructive phases, the resulting wave amplitude will spike. In software, this is fine. In hardware, this massive voltage spike will overdrive the RF-DACs and physically burn the Titanium Nitride traces on the BTO crystal.

$$\mathcal{L}_{thermo} = \text{ReLU}\left(\sqrt{Re_{phys}^2 + Im_{phys}^2} - \text{BTO\_LIMIT}\right)$$
3. Orthogonality Preservation ($\mathcal{L}_{HOPE}$)
The 256 frequencies traversing the core must remain mathematically orthogonal so they don't "bleed" meaning into each other (e.g., the system confusing the concept of "Apple" with "Red" because the wavelengths cross-modulated). We apply an orthogonality penalty to ensure the off-diagonal elements of the semantic correlation matrix remain at zero.
